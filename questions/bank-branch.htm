Bank Branch

Instructions

While most banks are striving to divert their customers away from branches to consume their digital banking services, many customers today still choose to visit the bank branch.

On a normal day, customers are patiently waiting in line at the bank branch, each waiting to be served by a branch officer. There are branch officers who can be determined from a collection of whole numbers given to you. [2, 3, 10, 4] would give you B = 4 branch officers working that day, and each branch officer can be uniquely identified by their id, which is the index + 1 of each element in that collection. Branch officers have their own X amount of serving time and their serving time can be determined by the element in the collection.

For example, the first branch officer has X = 2, the second branch officer has X = 3, the third branch officer has X = 10 and the fourth branch officer has X = 4. Each branch officer can only serve 1 customer at a time. When the bank first opens and/or when there are multiple available branch officers, the customer at the head of the queue will proceed to the lowest numbered branch officer. Once a branch officer finishes serving a customer, he immediately serves the next customer in line. When no branch officers are available, the customer next in line will have to wait until the next branch officer is available.

You are the Nth customer in the queue, determine the id of the branch officer that will serve you.

Part 1 Constraints (Peaceful Morning)

1 <= B <= 100

1 <= X <= 20

1 <= N <= 1000
